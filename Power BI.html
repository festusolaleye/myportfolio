Power BI Projects

This Bitcoin project analyzes historical market data from January 2012 to March 2021, focusing on minute-by-minute updates from select exchanges. Data cleaning and transformation ensured accuracy, addressing missing or erroneous entries. The analysis aims to uncover trends and patterns in Bitcoin market activity.

Key Insights:
Bitcoin's volume is 33.69 million, with a currency volume of 150.92 billion. The average weighted price is $4.47k. There's a progressive price trend in 2021, but it has the lowest BTC volume, indicating potentially lower trading activity. However, 2021 records the highest currency volume, signifying increased market participation.

Recommendation
There should be monitoring and addressing of any potential market volatility or fluctuations in currency volume to maintain stability and investor confidence in Bitcoin transactions.

This project analyzes historical stock prices of 10 popular companies from 2015 to 2021, including Apple, Amazon, Netflix, Microsoft, Google, Facebook, Tesla, Walmart, Uber, and Zoom. Thorough data cleaning ensured accuracy, followed by transformation techniques for analysis preparation. Visualizations were then created to depict trends and patterns in the stock price data, aiding in company comparison and informing predictive modeling for future stock prices.

Key Insights: The total stock value for the 10 companies is $487 billion. Aggregate opening prices amount to $6.94 million, while highs total $7.01 million and lows $6.85 million. Apple leads in average trading volume, while Amazon tops in average open prices. 2020 exhibits the highest volume trend, indicating significant trading activity and potential market volatility.

Recommendation
There should be an implementation of strategies to capitalize on the high average open prices of Amazon's stock and leverage the heightened volume trend observed in 2020 for more informed investment decisions.

This project delves into the realm of market basket analysis using retail sales data from 10 different shopping malls in Istanbul spanning the years 2021 to 2023. The dataset encompasses a wide range of attributes including invoice numbers, customer IDs, age, gender, payment methods, product categories, quantity, price, order dates, and shopping mall locations. Data preprocessing and cleaning were conducted to ensure data quality, leading to visualization to derive actionable insights.

Key Insights
The dataset includes data from around 99,000 customers across 10 Istanbul shopping malls, reflecting significant retail activity.
Total sales revenue amounts to $251.51 million, with 299,000 product units sold. Clothing emerges as the top revenue-generating and highest-selling category, indicating strong consumer demand.
October 2021 records the highest revenue, suggesting a peak in consumer spending or promotional activity.

Recommendation
Retailers should strategically plan future promotional events or marketing campaigns to replicate success and maximize revenue generation during peak months.

This project examines trade and finance data from the UNDP for 193 countries, focusing on indicators like exports, imports, foreign direct investment, and remittances in 2019. After preprocessing to handle missing values and ensure consistency, statistical analysis and visualization techniques were used to extract insights into global trade and finance trends.

Key Insights
The dataset covers trade and finance indicators for 193 countries over 14 years, offering a comprehensive view of global economic activity.
Hong Kong, China (SAR) emerges as the top performer, indicating its significant role in international trade and finance.
2010 stands out with the highest trend in exports and imports, reflecting heightened global economic activity.
Hong Kong, China (SAR) also leads in average exports and imports, highlighting its status as a major international trade hub.

Governments and organizations should invest in trade infrastructure, including ports, logistics networks, and trade facilitation services, to support efficient and seamless trade activities. Improved infrastructure can enhance the competitiveness of countries in the global market and attract more trade opportunities.

This project involves analyzing over 6,000,000 commercial airline flights from 2015, sourced from the U.S. DOT Air Travel Consumer Report. The dataset includes details such as airline names, flight numbers, origin and destination airports, flight distances, and scheduled/actual departure and arrival times. I ensured data integrity by handling missing values, formatting inconsistencies, and outlier detection, before visualization.

Key Insights
The dataset comprises over 6 million flights, with approximately 90,000 cancellations recorded.
Among the 14 airlines in the dataset, WN had the highest number of cancellations, while HA had the lowest.
WN also had the highest number of delayed flights, with Sunday having the highest number of delayed flights overall.

Recommendation
Given the significant number of cancelled and delayed flights, airlines should focus on improving operational efficiency and resilience to minimize disruptions. This may involve investing in better scheduling algorithms, proactive maintenance, and contingency planning to mitigate the impact of cancellations and delays on passengers.
